Chemical Signatures
i
✕ Chemical Signature Analysis

This section identifies molecular fragments that are statistically enriched within each Mechanism of Action (MOA) class in the training dataset.

How signatures are discovered:

• Each molecule is converted into circular fingerprints (ECFP, radius = 2).
• Each fingerprint bit corresponds to a specific local structural fragment.
• For every MOA class, the frequency of each fragment is compared against all other classes.
• An enrichment score is calculated:
(fragment frequency in class − fragment frequency outside class).

Interpretation:

• Positive enrichment → fragment is more common in that MOA class
• Higher enrichment score → stronger association with that mechanism
• These fragments represent structural patterns characteristic of that biological mechanism

Chemical signatures help explain which structural motifs are typically associated with different pharmacological behaviors.
